Progressive Equity Calculator

This tool estimates the payouts that founders and employees would receive
in an exit under a progressive equity scheme. The idea behind progressive
equity is to allow founders and early employees to share some of the wealth
created by a big exit with employees that have smaller holdings.

It was popularized in this article by Groupon's co-founder, Andrew Mason:
Introducing Progressive Equity

You can also follow the Hacker News Thread on the subject.

How it Works

Once a founder and/or employee has reached a pre-set "financial independence number",
additional earnings are taxed (usually 50%) and put into a kicker pool. This pool
is then redistributed to the remaining employees on a pro-rate basis.

In order to receive monies from the kicker pool, an individual must still be
an employee at the time of the exit. Former employees will be subject to the
tax, but not the redistribution. Investors are not subject to progressive equity.

Exit Price

Enter the estimated sale price of the company.

Financial Independence Number

Once a founder/employee reaches this number, additional earnings from
will be taxed at the rate specified below. eg. If your number is $15mm
and, with your equity, you would earn $18mm, $1.5mm will be put
into a kicker pool and you will receive $16.5mm instead.

Equity Tax

Earnings above the financial independence number will be taxed at
this rate and put into the kicker pool. Enter as a decimal eg.0.5

Equity Holders

Enter comma seperated list with the names of the equity holders.
You can list each individual seperately:
Example: investor_1, investor_2, founder_1, founder_2, employee_1, employee_2,...

Or you can group together the investors and employees with small holdings:
Example: investors, founder_1, founder_2, employee_1, employees

Equity Stake

Enter comma seperated list with the equity stake corresponding to each individual/group listed above.
Example: 0.1, 0.1, 0.25, 0.25, 0.1, 0.1, 0.05, 0.05

Subject to Progressive Equity

Specify True/False whether the person/group could be subject to progressive equity tax.
Generally, investors will always be listed as False and employees/founders as True.
Example: False, False, True, True, True, True, True, True

Currently Employed

Specify True/False whether the person/group is currently employed.
Only employees are eligible to receive a redistribution. Set investors to False.
Example: False, False, True, True, True, False, True, False
