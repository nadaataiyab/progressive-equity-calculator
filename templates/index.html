Progressive Equity Calculator

This tool calculates potential payouts to founders and employees under a progressive equity scheme. The idea behind progressive equity is to allow founders and early employees to share wealth created by a very large exit with employees that have smaller holdings.

It was popularized in this article by Groupon's co-founder, Andrew Mason:
Introducing Progressive Equity

You can also follow the Hacker News Thread on the subject.

How it Works

Once a founder and/or employee has reached a pre-set "financial independence number", additional earnings are taxed (usually at 50%) and put into a kicker pool. This pool is distributed to the remaining employees on a pro-rata basis.

For example, if the "financial independence number" is set to $15mm and the company sells for $100mm. A founder owning 25% of the company will receive $20mm instead of $25mm. The remaining $5mm will fund the kicker pool and be distributed among the rest of the employees.

As an employee your share of the kicker pool is equal to your equity holdings divided by the total equity owned by all the eligible employees times the kicker pool. For example if you owned 1% and all the eligible employees together owned 20%, you would get 1/20th of the $5mm kicker pool, equal to $250,000, on top of the $1mm you would otherwise receive in a $100mm exit.

In order to receive monies from the kicker pool, an individual must still be an employee at the time of the exit. Former employees will be subject to the tax, but not the redistribution. Investors are not subject to progressive equity, unless you somehow talked them into it.
